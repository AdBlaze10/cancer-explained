Why this matters

Cancer isn't just one disease. It's a collection of conditions caused by changes in how cells grow and behave. Understanding the basic biology helps us see why early detection, treatment, and prevention are so important.

How to learn this

Start with the basics of cells and DNA, then move into how mutations affect cell behavior. Each section builds on the previous one, so understanding the early ideas makes the later topics much easier.

Learning tips

Don't try to memorize everything at once. Focus on understanding how cells normally behave first. Then look at what changes in cancer. If something feels confusing, go back one step and build from there.

What's included

Big idea

At its core, cancer happens when normal cell controls stop working. Cells divide when they shouldn't, ignore signals to stop, and sometimes spread to other parts of the body.

Real-world connection

Every cancer treatment - from chemotherapy to immunotherapy, targets specific problems in how cells grow or survive. Learning these concepts helps explain how these treatments actually work.
